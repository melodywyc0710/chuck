Activity 7: Extended Response — Evaluate a Final Design

A7
Marks available12 marks
Read the brief and final design. Write an extended evaluation (5–6 sentences) covering purpose, audience, and constraints, with a balanced judgement.

Brief:

Design packaging for a children's snack brand. Audience: parents purchasing for children aged 4–8. Purpose: stand out on a crowded shelf while reassuring parents of healthy ingredients. Constraint: must be produced from recyclable cardboard, budget under $0.50 per unit.

Final Design:

The packaging uses bright, playful illustrated characters with a bold sans-serif logotype. A small "100% recyclable" icon and ingredient callouts appear on the front. It is printed using two spot colours on uncoated recycled card.

✅ Sample response (refer to after completing)

The final design largely meets the brief. The bright, playful illustrated characters and bold logotype are well suited to standing out on a crowded shelf, directly addressing the purpose of shelf impact for the child-facing side of the audience. The front-of-pack "100% recyclable" icon and ingredient callouts speak directly to the parent side of the audience, reassuring them of healthy ingredients and sustainability at the point of purchase. The use of uncoated recycled card and a limited two-spot-colour print process satisfies both the recyclability and budget constraints, keeping production costs low while remaining visually engaging. One limitation is that relying on illustrated characters alone may not clearly differentiate flavour variants across a product range — a secondary colour-coding system could further support parents making quick purchasing decisions.
