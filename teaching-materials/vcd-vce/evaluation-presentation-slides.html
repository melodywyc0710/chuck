Introduction
From Resolution to Final Presentation

Once a concept is resolved, it undergoes final refinement — small, precise adjustments — before being presented to a professional standard.

The finished design is then evaluated against the original brief to determine its success.

Key Skill
Presentation Standards
The final design must be presented using an appropriate method to a professional standard, clearly communicating the resolved concept.
Accuracy — correct use of orthogonal, isometric or perspective methods
Rendering — colour, tone and material communicated clearly
Context — mockups or environment shown where relevant
Craftsmanship — clean linework, consistent scale, legible annotation

See the Technical Drawing and Visualisation Drawing handouts (Unit 1 AOS 2) for a full recap of drawing methods.

Key Concept
Evaluating Against the Brief
Purpose
Does the design achieve its communication objective?
Audience
Is it appropriate and effective for the target audience?
Constraints
Were the stated constraints respected (materials, time, format)?
Obligatory Fields
Were all required design outcomes delivered?

A strong evaluation is specific and balanced — identify genuine strengths and any limitations, not just praise.

Key Skill
Folio Layout & Clarity
How your folio is organised affects how clearly your design thinking can be assessed.
Sequence — brief → research → generation → development → resolution → evaluation
Labelling — clearly label the brief, each concept, and final design
Consistency — consistent visual presentation across pages
Exam Tip
Exam questions may show a folio excerpt and ask you to identify which stage of the process it represents — know the full sequence well.
Exam Technique
Writing an Evaluation
Criterion → Evidence → Judgement
1. State the brief criterion being evaluated
2. Give evidence from the final design
3. Judge whether it succeeds, with reasoning
Sentence Starters
"The brief required [X]; the final design achieves this by..."
"This effectively meets the needs of [audience] because..."
"One limitation of the final design is..."
