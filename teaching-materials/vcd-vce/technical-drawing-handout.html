Technical Drawing Methods

VCE Visual Communication Design · Unit 1 AOS 2 · VCAA 2024

VCAA-VCD-DRAW1
Technical drawing uses standardised methods and conventions to represent objects accurately in two and three dimensions. In VCE VCD, technical drawings are used in design fields such as industrial design (products), environmental design (spaces), and communication design (layouts, diagrams). You need to know when and why each method is used.

📐 Orthogonal Drawing

aka Orthographic Projection · Working Drawing

Front, side, and top views on a single plane

Shows an object from multiple views (front, side, top, bottom) as flat, 2D projections. All lines are parallel — no perspective distortion. Uses true measurements.
Used when:
Manufacturing, engineering, product construction. Builders and manufacturers use these to build accurate 3D objects from 2D drawings.
Key termsFirst-angle projection · Third-angle projection · Sectional view · Hidden detail lines (dashed) · Dimension lines · Scale

🧊 Isometric Drawing

Type of axonometric / paraline drawing

3 faces visible, all at 30° to horizontal

3D drawing where all vertical lines are vertical and all horizontal lines are drawn at 30° to the horizontal. Edges remain at true length — no perspective foreshortening. Shows 3 faces simultaneously.
Used when:
Presenting product designs, architectural concepts, interior layouts. Accurate yet visually three-dimensional — useful for design development and client presentations.
Key termsIsometric grid · 30° angle · Axonometric · True length · No vanishing point · Receding lines at 30°

🎯 One-Point Perspective

Linear perspective · Single vanishing point

All lines converge to one vanishing point

A perspective drawing with one vanishing point on the horizon line. Horizontal and vertical lines remain parallel; lines going into the distance converge at the VP. Creates a sense of depth and realism.
Used when:
Interior design, corridor views, streetscapes, room layouts. Best for showing a straight-on view into a space — creates strong sense of depth and invitation into the scene.
Key termsVanishing point (VP) · Horizon line / Eye level · Receding lines · Picture plane · Depth

🏗️ Two-Point Perspective

Linear perspective · Two vanishing points

Two vanishing points, one vertical edge

A perspective drawing with two vanishing points on the horizon line. Vertical lines remain vertical; all other lines recede to one of the two VPs. Shows a corner view of an object or building.
Used when:
Product design (showing a corner), architectural exteriors, environmental design concepts, furniture design. More dynamic and realistic than one-point for most objects.
Key termsTwo vanishing points (VP1, VP2) · Horizon line · Vertical lines stay vertical · Receding planes · Corner view
