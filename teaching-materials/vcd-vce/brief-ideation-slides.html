Introduction
Writing Your Own Brief

Using your Unit 3 AOS 1 analysis and reinterpretation, you write a design brief that will guide your folio work across Units 3 and 4.

This brief must be specific enough to guide two distinct concepts, but open enough to allow genuine creative exploration.

Your Brief Must State
Client / context
Target audience
Purpose & communication objectives
Constraints (time, materials, format)
Two (2) obligatory design fields/outcomes
Key Skill
Deconstructing a Brief
Before generating ideas, break the brief into its component parts to ensure nothing is missed.
Who — the target audience and their needs
What — the required outcomes (obligatory design fields)
Why — the purpose and desired effect
Constraints — what limits the possible solutions
Exam Tip
Exam questions often ask you to identify a missing or unclear part of a given brief — practice spotting gaps in audience, purpose or constraints.
Key Requirement
Generating Two Distinct Concepts
Your folio must show a genuine range of ideas — leading to two distinctly different concepts that will each be developed and refined in Unit 4.
What Makes Concepts "Distinct"?
Different visual approach (not just colour changes)
Different treatment of elements/principles
Different overall style or design field emphasis
Ideation Methods (recap)
Brainstorming, mind mapping, thumbnail sketches, mood boards, SCAMPER — see the Unit 1 AOS 2 Design Process materials for a full recap of these methods.
Key Skill
Research to Support Ideation
Targeted research ensures your ideas are grounded in the brief's real audience and context, not just personal preference.
Audience research — interviews, surveys of the target group
Visual research — mood boards, existing designs in the same field
Contextual research — relevant trends, industry practice, materials

Link your research directly back to Unit 2 concepts — current trends and industry connections should inform your ideas.

Exam Technique
Answering Brief Questions
Identify → Explain → Suggest
1. Identify the missing/unclear brief element
2. Explain why it matters for the design outcome
3. Suggest what information should be added
Sentence Starters
"The brief does not specify [element], which is important because..."
"To generate ideas, [method] could be used to..."
"These two concepts differ in that..."
