Development & Resolution

VCE Visual Communication Design · Unit 4 AOS 1 · VCAA 2024

VCAA-VCD-DEV1
In Unit 4, you take the two distinct concepts generated in Unit 3 and develop each through testing, refinement and feedback. One concept is ultimately selected and resolved into a final presentation — but both must show visible development.
Methods of Development
Type Trials
Testing typefaces, weights and sizes for the strongest hierarchy and tone.
Colour Trials
Testing palettes for mood, contrast and audience appropriateness.
Material/Scale Trials
Testing physical materials, surface finishes or scale relationships.
Layout Trials
Testing composition, grid structure and hierarchy arrangements.

Every trial should be annotated — explain why an option was chosen or rejected, referencing the brief.

Feedback & Testing Against the Brief
Peer feedback
Classmates critique clarity and effectiveness.
Teacher feedback
Checks against assessment criteria.
Self-testing
Does it meet the audience, purpose and constraints?
Selecting a Direction to Resolve
  • Which concept best meets the audience & purpose stated in the brief?
  • Which received the strongest feedback from peers/teacher?
  • Which is most achievable within the constraints (time, materials)?

The rejected concept is not wasted — it still demonstrates breadth of development and is assessed as part of your folio.

Exam Sentence Starters

"Following feedback, the [feature] was changed to..."
"This concept was selected for resolution because..."
"The before/after comparison shows..."
