Trends in Design

VCE Visual Communication Design · Unit 2 AOS 1 · VCAA 2024

VCAA-VCD-TREND1
A design trend is a general direction in which design styles, materials or ideas are developing at a particular time. Trends emerge across all four design fields at once and are driven by broader forces in society. A trend is different from a fad, which is short-lived and stylistic only.

Three Current Design Trends (with examples across fields)

♻️ Sustainability

Driven by: environmental, social
A shift toward materials and processes that minimise environmental impact.
  • Comm: recycled stock, digital-first
  • Env: passive solar, green roofs
  • Ind: biodegradable packaging
  • Int: dark-mode, low-carbon web

🤖 Digital & AI

Driven by: technological
The rise of AI tools, generative design and immersive technology.
  • Comm: AI-generated imagery
  • Env: parametric architecture
  • Ind: generative product design
  • Int: AR/VR & voice interfaces

🕰️ Nostalgia

Driven by: cultural, social
A return to visual styles from past decades, reinterpreted for today.
  • Comm: Y2K type & palettes
  • Env: mid-century interiors
  • Ind: retro-styled products
  • Int: skeuomorphic UI
Trend vs Fad
TrendFad
DurationDevelops over years, reflects deeper shiftsShort-lived, often gone within a season
CauseSocial, cultural, economic, technological or environmental factorsNovelty, media hype, celebrity influence
ScopeAppears across multiple design fieldsUsually confined to one product or style
