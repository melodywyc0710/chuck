Activity 7: Extended Response — Evaluate a Brief

A7
Marks available10 marks
Read the brief and final design description below. Write an extended response (approx. 4–5 sentences) evaluating whether the design meets the brief. Refer to the audience, purpose, and constraints.

Brief:

Design a wayfinding sign system for a busy metro train station. The audience includes commuters, tourists and people with vision impairment. The client requires signage that is legible from a distance, works in low light, and can be produced cheaply in large quantities.

Final Design:

The final signs use large, high-contrast sans-serif type in white on a dark navy background, with universal pictograms alongside text. Signs are printed on durable vinyl and mounted at eye level with reflective coating.

✅ Sample response (refer to after completing)

The design largely meets the brief. The high-contrast white-on-navy type addresses the requirement for legibility in low light and from a distance, directly serving commuters and tourists moving quickly through the station. The inclusion of universal pictograms alongside text supports the accessibility needs of the audience, including tourists who may not read English fluently and people with vision impairment who rely on strong tonal contrast and symbol recognition rather than text alone. The choice of durable vinyl with reflective coating satisfies the constraint of cheap, large-scale production while maintaining longevity in a high-traffic public environment. One area for further development would be testing the pictograms with vision-impaired users specifically, to confirm the design's effectiveness for that audience segment.
