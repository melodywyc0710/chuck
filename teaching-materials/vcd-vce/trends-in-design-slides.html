Introduction
What is a Design Trend?

A design trend is a general direction in which design styles, materials or ideas are developing at a particular time.

Trends emerge across all four design fields — communication, environmental, industrial and interaction design — often at the same time, driven by shared cultural forces.

Trend vs Fad — a trend develops over a longer period and reflects deeper shifts; a fad is short-lived and often stylistic only.

Why Study Trends?
Understand the context a design was created in
Predict future audience expectations
Position your own folio work within current practice
Analyse why a design looks the way it does
Key Concept
Factors that Drive Trends
Trends don't appear from nowhere — they are shaped by broader forces acting on society. When analysing a trend, always link it back to one or more of these factors.

Example Trend 1
Sustainability & Eco-Design
A shift toward materials, processes and forms that minimise environmental impact — driven largely by environmental and social factors.
Across the 4 Fields
Communication — recycled paper stock, reduced ink use, digital-first campaigns
Environmental — passive solar buildings, green roofs, reclaimed materials
Industrial — biodegradable packaging, modular/repairable products
Interaction — dark-mode UI to reduce energy use, low-carbon web design
Example Trend 2
Digital & AI-Driven Design
The rise of AI tools, generative design and immersive technology — driven by technological factors and shifting audience expectations.
Across the 4 Fields
Communication — AI-generated imagery, motion graphics, personalised content
Environmental — parametric/generative architectural forms
Industrial — generative product design optimised by software
Interaction — AR/VR interfaces, voice UI, adaptive apps
Analysis Tip
When discussing this trend in the exam, name the specific technology (e.g. generative AI, parametric modelling) rather than saying "modern design" — vague language earns fewer marks.
Example Trend 3
Nostalgia & Retro Revival
A return to visual styles from past decades — driven by cultural and social factors such as comfort-seeking during uncertain times.
Across the 4 Fields
Communication — 90s/Y2K typography and colour palettes returning in branding
Environmental — mid-century modern interiors revived
Industrial — retro-styled appliances and vehicles
Interaction — skeuomorphic UI elements referencing physical objects
Trend Cycles
Trends are often cyclical — styles from 20–30 years ago frequently resurface, reinterpreted with current technology and values.
Skill
Analysing a Trend
Trend → Factor(s) → Field Examples
1. Name the trend
2. Identify the driving factor(s)
3. Give evidence from at least two design fields
Exam vocab: emerging, established, resurgence, driven by, reflects, in response to, across fields
Sentence Starters
"This trend is driven primarily by [factor] because..."
"In communication design, this trend manifests as..."
"Compared to [past trend], this reflects a shift toward..."
"This trend is likely to continue/fade because..."
