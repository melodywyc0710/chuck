Analysis & Reinterpretation

VCE Visual Communication Design · Unit 3 AOS 1 · VCAA 2024

VCAA-VCD-ANLY1
In Unit 3, you select an existing design from one of the four design fields, analyse it in depth, then reinterpret it for a new context, audience or purpose — forming the foundation for your folio's two developed concepts.
Analysis Framework
Purpose & Audience
Who was the design made for, and what was it intended to achieve?
Elements & Principles
What visual language is used, and what effect does it have on the viewer?
Materials & Methods
How was the design produced, and what does this reveal about its context?
Context
When/where was it created; what design field or movement does it belong to?
Reconceptualisation vs Reinvention

Reconceptualisation

Changing the design for a new audience, purpose or context while keeping its core visual identity recognisable.
e.g. redesigning a children's brand identity for an adult luxury market

Reinvention

A more radical transformation — taking the underlying idea or function and expressing it in an entirely new visual form or medium.
e.g. reinventing a print poster campaign as an interactive app experience
Methods of Reinterpretation
Change Audience
Redesign for a different age group, culture or market segment.
Change Field
Move a concept from one design field to another.
Change Era/Style
Reimagine using a different historical or contemporary style.
Change Medium
Translate a static design into digital, interactive or 3D form.
Exam Sentence Starters
"The original design uses [element/principle] to..."
"If reconceptualised for [new audience], this could..."
"Reinventing this in [new field/medium] would require..."
"This reflects the design's original context because..."
