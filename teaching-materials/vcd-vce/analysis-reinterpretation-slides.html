Introduction
Analysing & Reinterpreting Designs

In Unit 3, you select an existing design from one of the four design fields, analyse it in depth, then reinterpret it for a new context, audience or purpose.

This process forms the foundation for your folio — the analysis and reinterpretation informs the two concepts you will develop across Units 3 and 4.

The Two Stages
1. Analysis — understanding the original design in depth
2. Reinterpretation — reimagining it for a different context
Key Skill
A Framework for Analysis
A thorough analysis considers more than just appearance — it examines the design's full context of creation and use.
Purpose & audience — who was it made for, and why?
Elements & principles — what visual language is used, and to what effect?
Materials & methods — how was it made or produced?
Context — when/where was it created; what design field and movement does it belong to?
Exam Tip
Always support analysis with specific visual evidence from the design — not general impressions. Use the Name → Evidence → Effect formula.
Key Concept
Reconceptualisation vs Reinvention
Reconceptualisation
Changing the design for a new audience, purpose or context while keeping its core visual identity recognisable.
e.g. redesigning a children's brand identity for an adult luxury market
Reinvention
A more radical transformation — taking the underlying idea or function of a design and expressing it in an entirely new visual form or medium.
e.g. reinventing a print poster campaign as an interactive app experience

Both approaches must be clearly justified — explain why the change suits the new audience/purpose, not just what changed.

Methods
Ways to Reinterpret a Design
Change Audience
Redesign for a different age group, culture, or market segment.
Change Field
Move a concept from one design field to another (e.g. a logo to a spatial installation).
Change Era/Style
Reimagine a historical design using a contemporary visual style, or vice versa.
Change Medium
Translate a static design into digital, interactive or 3D form.
Exam vocab: reconceptualise, reinvent, reinterpret, contemporary context, target audience shift, visual identity, transform
Exam Technique
Answering Analysis Questions
Feature → Evidence → Effect → Context
1. Name a visual feature (element/principle)
2. Describe specific evidence in the design
3. Explain the effect on the audience
4. Link to the design's context or purpose
Sentence Starters
"The original design uses [element/principle] to..."
"If reconceptualised for [new audience], this could..."
"Reinventing this in [new field/medium] would require..."
Summary
Analysis & Reinterpretation — Quick Reference
Analysis
Purpose, audience, elements/principles, materials, context.
Reconceptualise
Same core identity, new audience/purpose.
Reinvent
Radical transformation of form/medium.
Justify
Always explain why the change suits the new context.
