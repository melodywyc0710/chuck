Design & Industry Connections

Real design projects rarely sit inside a single field. Designers collaborate with clients, manufacturers, other designers and tradespeople, and work within broader industry factors such as sustainability, technology, ethics and intellectual property (IP) law.
Who Designers Collaborate With

🤝 Clients

Provide the design brief, approve directions, and fund the project. The designer must balance the client's goals with the audience's needs.

🏗️ Manufacturers & Builders

Turn a design into physical reality — printers, factories, construction trades. Their capabilities and constraints shape what's possible.

🎨 Other Designers

A single project often needs several specialists, e.g. a graphic designer working alongside a UX/interaction designer or architect.

📣 Marketing & Developers

Deliver the finished design to its final audience — through campaigns, code, packaging, or distribution.
Factors Shaping Industry Practice
🌱 Sustainability
Materials, energy use, waste and end-of-life disposal are now standard client requirements.
💻 Technology
New software, materials and manufacturing methods change what's possible and expected.
⚖️ Ethics
Fair labour, honest advertising, accessibility and inclusive design are professional expectations.
©️ Intellectual Property
Copyright, trademarks and licensing protect original design work from unauthorised copying.
