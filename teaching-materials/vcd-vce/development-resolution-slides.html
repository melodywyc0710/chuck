Introduction
Developing Your Two Concepts

In Unit 4, you take the two distinct concepts generated in Unit 3 and develop each one further through testing, refinement and feedback.

One concept is ultimately selected and resolved into a final presentation — but both must show visible development.

Development Shows
Testing of options (type, colour, material)
Annotated decision-making
Response to feedback
Progress toward a resolved final design
Key Methods
Methods of Development
Type Trials
Testing typefaces, weights and sizes for the strongest hierarchy and tone.
Colour Trials
Testing palettes for mood, contrast and audience appropriateness.
Material/Scale Trials
Testing physical materials, surface finishes or scale relationships.
Layout Trials
Testing composition, grid structure and hierarchy arrangements.

Every trial should be annotated — explain why an option was chosen or rejected, referencing the brief.

Key Concept
Feedback & Testing Against the Brief
Development is not just personal preference — it is an iterative process of testing ideas against the brief and gathering feedback.
Peer feedback — classmates critique clarity and effectiveness
Teacher feedback — checks against assessment criteria
Self-testing — does it meet the audience, purpose and constraints?
Exam Tip
When describing development, always show the before → feedback/testing → after sequence, not just the final result.
Key Skill
Selecting a Direction to Resolve
After development, one concept is chosen to be resolved into the final design. This decision must be justified against the brief.
Which concept best meets the audience & purpose?
Which received the strongest feedback?
Which is most achievable within constraints (time, materials)?

The rejected concept is not wasted — it still demonstrates breadth of development and is assessed as part of your folio.

Exam Technique
Answering Development Questions
Method → Change → Justification
1. Name the development method used
2. Describe what changed between versions
3. Justify the change against the brief
Sentence Starters
"A colour trial was conducted to test..."
"Following feedback, the [feature] was changed to..."
"This concept was selected for resolution because..."
