Activity 7: Extended Response — Case Study

A7
Marks available10 marks
Read the case study. Write an extended response (4–5 sentences) analysing the original design and proposing ONE method of reinterpretation, with justification.

A 1980s arcade game box uses bold primary colours, chunky pixel-art illustration and a thick black outline typeface. It was designed to appeal to teenage boys in video rental stores.

✅ Sample response (refer to after completing)

The original design uses bold primary colours and a thick black outline typeface to create high contrast and energetic visual impact, appealing to its teenage audience through a sense of excitement and action appropriate to the arcade genre. The chunky pixel-art illustration reflects the technological context of the era, when low-resolution graphics were the industry standard. If reconceptualised for a contemporary retro-gaming subscription service targeting nostalgic adults, the same pixel-art style and bold palette could be retained, but refined with cleaner vector linework and a more sophisticated colour saturation — preserving the nostalgic identity while signalling a premium, adult-oriented product rather than a children's toy-store item.
