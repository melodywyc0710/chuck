Introduction
Design Fields Overlap & Connect

Real design projects rarely sit inside a single field. A single product often requires multiple design fields working together.

Example: launching a café involves communication design (branding), environmental design (fit-out), industrial design (furniture) and interaction design (ordering app) — often coordinated by different specialists.

Why This Matters
Designers must collaborate across disciplines
Understand how their design fits a bigger production system
Work within industry standards, ethics and IP law
Key Concept
Collaboration in Industry
Designers rarely work in isolation. They collaborate with other professionals and tradespeople to bring a design from concept to reality.
Who Designers Collaborate With
Clients — provide the brief, approve directions, fund the project
Manufacturers / builders — turn designs into physical reality
Other designers — e.g. a graphic designer working with a UX designer
Marketing / printers / developers — deliver the design to its final audience
Why Collaboration Matters
No single designer has expertise in every stage — from materials science to code to construction. Good collaboration keeps the design's original intent intact as it moves through production.
Key Concept
Factors Shaping Industry Practice
🌱 Sustainability
Materials, energy use, waste and end-of-life disposal are now standard client requirements.
💻 Technology
New software, materials and manufacturing (3D printing, AI tools) change what's possible and expected.
⚖️ Ethics
Fair labour, honest advertising, accessibility and inclusive design are professional expectations.
©️ Intellectual Property
Copyright, trademarks and licensing protect original design work from unauthorised copying.

These factors don't just affect big companies — they shape every design decision, from the materials in your VCD folio model to the fonts you're licensed to use.

Deep Dive
Intellectual Property (IP)
Intellectual property law protects original creative and design work from being copied or used without permission.
Types Relevant to Designers
Copyright — automatically protects original creative works (images, layouts, text)
Trademark — protects brand names, logos and slogans
Design registration — protects the visual appearance of a manufactured product
Patent — protects a new invention or functional process
Why It Matters to You
In your own folio, always use your own photography/imagery or correctly licensed stock and fonts — using copyrighted material without permission is a real industry issue, not just a school rule.
Exam Technique
Answering Industry Connection Questions
Connection → Roles → Factor
1. Identify which design fields/roles are connected
2. Explain how they collaborate
3. Link to a factor (sustainability, technology, ethics, IP)
Sentence Starters

"The designer collaborated with [role] to..."
"This decision reflects growing industry concern for [factor]..."
"Without protection of [IP type], this design could be..."
