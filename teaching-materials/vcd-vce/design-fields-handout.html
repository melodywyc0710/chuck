Design Fields

What is a design field? A design field is a specialised area of practice within visual communication design. The VCAA 2024 Study Design identifies four design fields. Each field has distinct purposes, audiences, materials, methods, and professional contexts. Understanding these fields helps you contextualise design work in analysis and justify choices in your own design practice.

📢 Communication Design

aka Graphic Design · Visual Communication
Designs that communicate messages, information, or ideas to audiences through visual media. Primarily 2D, print and digital. Focuses on legibility, clarity, and persuasion.

Examples of design outcomes:

  • Print: posters, brochures, magazines, packaging, book covers, annual reports
  • Digital: websites, apps, social media graphics, motion graphics, email campaigns
  • Brand: logos, brand identity systems, wayfinding/signage
  • Advertising: campaigns across print, outdoor, digital media
Related careersGraphic designer · Art director · Brand designer · UX/UI designer · Web designer · Typographer

🏙️ Environmental Design

aka Spatial Design · Interior & Landscape
Designs that shape built environments and spaces for function, experience, and aesthetic. Considers how people move through and interact with physical spaces.

Examples of design outcomes:

  • Interior: home interiors, retail spaces, hospitality design (cafés, hotels)
  • Exhibition: museum exhibitions, trade show stands, pop-up installations
  • Landscape: parks, streetscapes, urban plazas, playgrounds
  • Wayfinding: signage systems for airports, hospitals, campuses
Related careersInterior designer · Landscape architect · Exhibition designer · Urban designer · Set designer

⚙️ Industrial Design

aka Product Design · Manufacturing Design
Designs for mass-produced or manufactured objects that combine function, ergonomics, aesthetics, and materials engineering. Considers how products are used, held, and produced at scale.

Examples of design outcomes:

  • Consumer: furniture, kitchen appliances, electronics, toys
  • Transport: car body design, bicycle components, public seating
  • Medical: prosthetics, medical devices, hospital equipment
  • Packaging: structural packaging (bottles, boxes, containers)
Related careersIndustrial designer · Product designer · Ergonomics specialist · Furniture designer · Packaging designer

💻 Interaction Design

aka UX Design · Digital Product Design
Designs that shape how users interact with digital systems, interfaces, and experiences. Prioritises usability, accessibility, and the quality of the user's journey through a product or service.

Examples of design outcomes:

  • Apps: mobile apps, software interfaces, dashboards
  • Web: website UX/UI, landing pages, e-commerce flows
  • Interactive: touchscreen kiosks, interactive installations, games
  • Systems: information architecture, design systems, chatbot interactions
Related careersUX designer · UI designer · Interaction designer · Service designer · Accessibility specialist
