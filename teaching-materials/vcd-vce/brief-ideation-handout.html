The Brief & Ideation

VCE Visual Communication Design · Unit 3 AOS 2 · VCAA 2024

VCAA-VCD-BRIEF1
Using your Unit 3 AOS 1 analysis and reinterpretation, you write a design brief to guide your folio work across Units 3 and 4. It must be specific enough to guide two distinct concepts, but open enough to allow genuine creative exploration.
Your Brief Checklist
☐ Client / context
☐ Target audience
☐ Purpose / communication objectives
☐ Constraints (time, materials, format)
☐ Two obligatory design outcomes/fields
☐ Success criteria for evaluation
Deconstructing a Brief
Who
The target audience and their specific needs.
What
The required outcomes — the obligatory design fields.
Why
The purpose and the desired effect on the audience.
Constraints
What limits the possible solutions (time, budget, materials).
Generating Two Distinct Concepts

Your folio must show a genuine range of ideas leading to two distinctly different concepts. Concepts are only "distinct" if they differ in visual approach, treatment of elements/principles, or overall style — not just colour changes.

Research to Support Ideation
Audience research
Interviews, surveys of the target group.
Visual research
Mood boards, existing designs in the same field.
Contextual research
Relevant trends and industry practice (links to Unit 2).
Ideation methods
Brainstorming, mind mapping, thumbnails, SCAMPER (see Unit 1 AOS 2).
Exam Sentence Starters
"The brief does not specify [element], which is important because..."
"To generate ideas, [method] could be used to..."
"These two concepts differ in that..."
"This research informs the design because..."
